About Me

         I am currently a final-year Ph.D. student of atmospheric science at USTC in China and working with Prof. Rui Li. I mainly study the hydrological variabilities in the terrestrial ecosystem using multiple methods including but not limited to satellite remote sensing, field works and the physical-based modelling. In particular, I develop algorithms of retrieving surface microwave emissivity and the associated vegetation index using passive microwave (PMW) remote sensing. The related databases benefit the endeavors in surface ecohydrological dynamics and the retrieval of over-land atmospheric parameters. My studies also involve plenty of technical questions, e.g., the PMW resolution-enhancement, satellite observational geometry simulation, and cross-calibration of sensors from multiplatforms.

         My current main project is the development of Microwave Radiative Transfer Model of multilayered canopy, so as to facilitate the retrieval of vertical hydrological structure of developed forest using satellite PMW multichannel observations. Validated with ground-based/truck-mounted radiometric measurements, the MODEL is expected to bridge the insights into the vegetation hydrodynamics from the scale of cohort/cluster to satellite footprint.

Research Interests

  • All-weather Microwave Land Surface Emissivity (MLSE)

    Retrieve MLSE under clear/cloudy skies using multisource satellite observations of passive microwave, VIS/Infrared, and renanalysis.

  • Multichannel Emissivity Difference Vegetation Index (EDVI)

    Investigate the spatial and temporal variabilities of multichannel EDVIs and their reponses to the vegetation hydrological dynamics.

  • Radiative Transfer Model(RTM) and Forward Emission Simulations

    Forward RTM development with optimal surface parameterizations, capable of describing the apparent radiance above multilayered VWC structure.
